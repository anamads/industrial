About Us

Most finishing work lives or dies on the preparation underneath it. Sanding isn't the enjoyable part - but it's the part that determines whether the end result looks right. It deserves better than a disc that loads up on the third pass and a pack that runs out before the job does.

Most of what's on the shelf is the same product in different packaging. Brown paper, average performance, and a pack count that runs out before the job does.

Life is too short for bad sandpaper.

M&O was built on the premise that the category could simply be done better - better materials, better specification, better value.

THE DESIGN

Hardware shelves tend to look the same: yellows, oranges, and browns competing for attention in the same visual language. M&O takes a different approach.

Good abrasives, it turns out, are black and white.

The black packaging is deliberate. It reads as calm and confident rather than busy - and it stands apart from the category without trying hard to do so. The white abrasive surface reinforces the same idea: a visible departure from convention that signals something considered rather than generic.

In a category that competes on colour and claims, M&O competes on what's inside the pack.
